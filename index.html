My Projects

  • Automatic Sandwich Maker

    Introduction

    This project was done for my 1A final project. The purpose of this project is to implement what we've been study into a real world situation. This machine is designed specifically for university students who don't have time to cook breakfast or launch; we build a machine that makes lunches for them. It is only a proven of concept, but still, it is designed and programmed by my teammates and me . Every piece of structure or program was built from scratch.






    Explanation

    For the actual product, we are aming to combime the food preservation system, food dispensing system and maybe the paying system for some special situations together. The idea is to ensure a fresh sandwich produced in less than 30 seconds with only a push of a button. To achieve those aspects, we designed the simplest yet most functional design that best suits our need.

    MechnicalComponents

    There are three main parts of this robot

    First, the dispensining mechanism. As you can see from the picture to the right, the two mortors are connected to the hand-crafted strings on each side with a dual-directional lock fixed in between them. The dual-directional separates the direction of turning of the two springs which redeces the amount of motors used(from 4 to 2).

    Second, the sliding mechanism at the bottom part of the robot. Once the ingridients are being pushed out by the springs, the collector fixed on a motor driven sliding plate will position itself at the middle ot the machine and collect the ingridients. After all ingridients are being collected, the slider will move to the edge of this machine and make it convinent to pick up by customers.

    Third, food preservation system. When the machine is not in use, all the food will be preserved in two of the transparent boxes fixed on top of this machine. Ideally, we are going to design them as a small customized fridge, however, considering the budget, this design is being abandoned.

  • project 1

    Introduction

    This is one of our most successful project. The entire team went to downtown hamilton to joint the Innovation Nation Robotics Contest. We ended up winning 200$ and placed third among all teams. The purpose of this competition is to build robot prototypes that would benefit society. Since we are in Canada and Canada is famous for its hockey, we decided to build a automatic zamboni.

    Explaination

    The picture on the right shows the moment when I was doing a demonstration to all judges and audiences about our mechinical components. As you can see, the back of the zamboni can be opened just like a real zamboni. The ice is going to be scrapped inside those storaging area by the spirals at the front of this zamboni. The scraped ice will go into the storage area where it gets melted and than the water is collected and sprayed onto the surface of the ice rink using the spraying system mounted at the back of this robot. The idea behind it is that there will be 6 identical robots working at the same time on the ice rink. They will be communicating with eachother and clean the ice rink much more efficiently(in roughly 3 min). This product would definitely replace the entire zamboni industry.

    Final Competition

    After all that many years, this becomes the last competition we joined as Thunder Ice robotics team. We challanged ourselves and made countless break throughs. Because of passion, and motivation, we were able to transform from 0 to 1. Here's the picture of the team after the competition.

  • project 5

    Introduction

    What you are seeing right now is the 1998 japanese Nitro Powered RC Buggy. The reason why I choose this 1998 design was to challange myself. I did end up building and modifying this buggy. It took me 30 days to build and 60 days to tune up the engine(the engine wwas old and fragile). It was functioning perfectly until I added some modifications and crushed into a tree.







    Engine

    Standard Nitro 2.0cc, hard to tune and hard to fix. Tuning is just to adjust the air and oil nozzle to a certain ratio so that the engine can run smoothly. I started out by just trying out every possible combinations that can be found online but failed. After that, I spent huge amount of time to just find out the pattern when I make specific changes to the nozzles. By hearing the sound of the engine running, I was able to eventually get it done. After two month of experience, I gained a better understanding of being patient.







    Suspension System

    The suspension system of this buggy was complete but not strong. It contains all the components such as differentials, shocks, but there were all made by plastic. I was trying to reenforce it by adding extra platings aroung it, but ended up complete useless.







    Differentials

    One last thing to point out was that the differentials on this buggy was broken, so I had to repair it manually.
